Ecosystem Science

10 lessons. 30 minutes each. Understand any environment from first principles.

Ages 12–15 10 Lessons 30 min each Interactive quizzes

Lessons

  • 01
    What Is a System? Ecosystem as input/output/feedback machine. Biotic vs abiotic components. The Yellowstone wolves → river shape story.
    Quiz
  • 02
    Energy: Where It All Starts Photosynthesis from atoms up. The 10% energy rule. Why top predators are always rare and food chains are short.
    Quiz
  • 03
    Food Webs: The Messy Reality Food chains are textbook fiction. Webs are real. Redundancy, keystone species (sea otters, wolves, elephants), trophic cascades.
    Quiz
  • 04
    Decomposers: Death Is Not the End Fungi, bacteria, detritivores. The carbon cycle. Without decomposers Earth drowns in unrotted corpses. Humus and why soil is irreplaceable.
    Quiz
  • 05
    The Nitrogen Cycle 78% of air is nitrogen. Almost nothing can use it. How bacteria fix, nitrify, and denitrify it — and why legumes are planted before wheat.
    Quiz
  • 06
    Aquatic Ecosystems: The Pond Photic zones, dissolved oxygen, phytoplankton as invisible forests. Eutrophication: when fertiliser kills a lake.
    Quiz
  • 07
    The Aquarium Nitrogen Cycle NH₃ → Nitrosomonas → NO₂ → Nitrobacter → NO₃. Why new tanks kill fish. How to cycle a 60L aquarium safely.
    Hands-on
  • 08
    Build a Terrarium All four cycles at once: water, carbon, nitrogen, energy. Design a self-sustaining closed terrarium and justify every component from first principles.
    Hands-on
  • 09
    Ocean Ecosystems 71% of Earth's surface. Photic zone. Chemosynthesis at hydrothermal vents — life without the sun. Whale falls as temporary ecosystems.
    Quiz
  • 10
    Resilience and Collapse Biodiversity is redundancy. What makes systems absorb shocks vs collapse? Extinction debt. Synthesise everything from Lessons 1–9.
    Quiz
