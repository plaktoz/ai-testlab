# Learning Record 0001: Ecosystem Fundamentals Baseline

**Date:** June 22, 2026

## What We Covered

Introduced the learner to the foundational concept of an ecosystem:
- Definition: A community of living things plus non-living environment, all interconnected
- Three key players: Producers (plants), Consumers (animals), Decomposers (recyclers)
- How energy flows through ecosystems in one direction (sun → producers → consumers → decomposers)
- Food chain as a simple model of energy transfer

## How It Was Taught

Started with a concrete, relatable forest scenario to hook curiosity. Used metaphor (sports team) to explain interdependence. Introduced vocabulary systematically with examples.

## Key Insights

The learner now understands that:
- Ecosystems are interconnected systems where removing one component affects others
- Energy sources (sun) and flows through organisms in predictable patterns
- Different organisms play different roles (producer/consumer/decomposer)

## Next Steps

Lesson 2 will introduce ecosystem diversity by comparing different ecosystem types (forest, pond, ocean) and showing how the same principles apply in different contexts.

## Notes for Future Sessions

- Started with forest as the primary example—relatable for most 12-year-olds
- The learner should do the outdoor challenge (identifying producers/consumers/decomposers) before Lesson 2 to build storage strength through real-world application
