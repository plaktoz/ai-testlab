📚 Ecosystem Glossary

Your Quick Reference Guide

Use this glossary to look up ecosystem terms. All lessons will use these exact definitions.

Carnivore
An animal that eats meat (other animals).
Example: Wolf, hawk, shark
Consumer
Any living thing that eats other organisms to get energy. Includes herbivores, carnivores, and omnivores.
Example: Deer, lion, butterfly, you!
Decomposer
An organism that breaks down dead plants and animals, returning nutrients to the soil.
Example: Fungus, bacteria, earthworm
Ecosystem
A community of living things (animals, plants, insects) plus the non-living things around them (soil, water, air, sunlight), all connected together by the flow of energy and materials.
Example: Forest, pond, ocean, coral reef
Energy Flow
The movement of energy from the sun through plants and animals in an ecosystem. Energy flows in one direction: sun → producer → consumer → decomposer.
Example: Sun energy → grass → rabbit → fox
Food Chain
A sequence showing how energy passes from one organism to the next, starting with a producer and moving through consumers.
Example: Plant → Grasshopper → Bird → Hawk
Food Web
A more complex version of a food chain that shows multiple interconnected food chains in an ecosystem.
Example: Plants eaten by multiple animals; one animal eating multiple types of food
Habitat
The specific place where an organism lives (its home and surroundings).
Example: Beaver's habitat is a river; squirrel's habitat is a forest
Herbivore
An animal that eats only plants.
Example: Rabbit, deer, elephant, caterpillar
Omnivore
An animal that eats both plants and meat.
Example: Bear, raccoon, human, chicken
Organism
Any living thing—a plant, animal, insect, fungus, or bacterium.
Example: Tree, dog, bee, mushroom
Photosynthesis
The process by which plants use sunlight, water, and air to make their own food (energy).
Example: A tree's leaves capture sunlight to grow
Producer
An organism (usually a plant) that creates energy from the sun using photosynthesis. Producers form the base of a food chain.
Example: Grass, tree, seaweed, algae
